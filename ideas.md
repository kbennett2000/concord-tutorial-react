# Ideas — what could you build?

You came in unable to read a React file. You leave having built five working apps and knowing you
can open a real one. So here's the only question left: what do you want to make?

None of these is an assignment. They're starting points — each one is mostly things you already did,
recombined. Pick the one that makes you a little curious, and steal freely from
[recipes.md](recipes.md).

## A verse of the day

Show a single verse, beautifully, and let it greet you each morning. *(Lesson 1.)* Start with one
fixed verse; then pick a different one each day from a little list of your favorites.

## Your own search page

The Bible search from Lesson 2, but yours — your styling, your default word, maybe a row of
one-tap searches for words you look up a lot. *(Lesson 2.)*

## A translation comparer

Lesson 3 lined up three translations. Show more — five, eight — and let the reader choose which
ones. *(Lesson 3.)* A good next step: let them add or remove a column without touching the code.

## A cross-reference explorer with a trail

Wander Scripture by its connections like in Lesson 4 — but keep a breadcrumb of where you've been,
so you can see the path you followed and step back. *(Lesson 4, plus a little array in state.)*

## A topical study tool

Combine two lessons: search a theme, pick a result, then follow *its* cross-references. One small
app that lets someone pull a thread through a whole topic. *(Lessons 2 and 4.)*

## A "find verses about…" tool

Use meaning search instead of keywords — the stretch from [recipes.md](recipes.md). Type an idea,
get the closest verses, ranked. It surprises people, in a good way.

## A scripture-memory helper

Show a verse, then hide a few words and let the reader fill them back in — or reveal them one tap
at a time. *(Lesson 1, plus a piece of state for what's hidden.)*

## Something for your people

A shared reading for a study group. A one-page handout that pulls a passage and its cross-references
for Sunday. A kiosk that shows a rotating verse. The most useful thing you build may be the small one
that helps the folks around you.

## Read songbird — then change one thing

The real graduation. Open [songbird](https://github.com/kbennett2000/songbird), find one small thing
— a label, a color, the number of cross-references it shows — and change it. The moment a real
project bends to *you*, the wall you started with is gone for good.

---

You don't need a plan or permission. Pick the smallest idea that pulls at you, open a file, and
start — you already know how. When you get stuck, [recipes.md](recipes.md) has the moves,
[songbird](https://github.com/kbennett2000/songbird) shows them at full size, and Concord's
[`docs/API.md`](https://github.com/kbennett2000/concord/blob/main/docs/API.md) lists everything it
can hand you.

You're a Concord builder. Go build something.
